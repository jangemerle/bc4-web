# Agentic Engineering: From Vibe Coding to Directing AI

**Source:** Luuk Alleman (YouTube) — "Stop Vibe Coding (Do This Instead)"
**Date:** 2026-03-11
**Added:** 2026-04-04

## Key Takeaways

- **Karpathy coined the next term:** "Agentic engineering" — you're not writing code 99% of the time, you're *directing agents who do*. The word that matters is *directing*.
- **The vibe coding ceiling:** Every prompt without a clear plan adds something you don't understand. It compounds quietly until nothing works and you don't know where to start.
- **Trust in AI code is dropping:** Stack Overflow 2025 survey — trust in AI-generated code fell from 40% to 29% in a single year.
- **Y Combinator signal:** 25% of YC 2025 batch had codebases 95% AI-generated. Vibe coding was always the starting point, not the destination.

## The Three Habits of Agentic Engineering

1. **Define the output before opening the tool** — Not the feature, the outcome. "What does success look like?" in two sentences → prompts become 10x more precise.
2. **Give the AI one job at a time** — Break builds into small, clear steps. Each verifiable before moving on.
3. **Review before moving on** — Test what was just built before prompting the next step. Eliminates "something broke and I don't know where."

## The Core Distinction

| Vibe Coder | Agentic Engineer |
|---|---|
| Works backwards from AI output | Works forwards from a clear picture |
| "Build me a dashboard" | "Dashboard showing 3 metrics, from this source, 30s refresh, loading state" |
| Describing | Defining |
