# Hey.com — Website Inspiration Analysis

**URL:** https://www.hey.com
**Added:** 2026-04-08
**Category:** Email/calendar product (37signals/Basecamp)

---

## Hero Strategy

**Headline:** "We finally fixed your email + calendar!"
**Subheadline:** "Gmail, Outlook, and Apple got complacent and took their eye off the ball. Then along came HEY."
**CTA:** "Try HEY free for 30-days" + microcopy "No obligation, no CC required."

**Rotating social proof ticker** above the headline — three praise quotes scroll before the user reads the main copy. Pre-loads credibility.

**Video demo embed** directly in hero: "How does HEY work?" — product visible immediately, not gated behind signup.

### Kvalt application
- **Confrontational positioning:** "Design systems were drowning in process. We cut the fat."
- **Rotating ticker above hero:** Short praise quotes scrolling before the headline. Pre-loads credibility.
- **Specificity in CTA:** "30 days" not "free trial." "No CC required" removes last objection.
- **Video/demo in hero:** Product visible immediately.

## Product Explanation

**Linear scroll narrative.** Strict pattern: bold heading, one paragraph, full-width screenshot. One concept per section:

1. "Screen emails like you screen your calls" — The Screener
2. "The Imbox is for your important email" — deliberate misspelling, memorable
3. "A Paper Trail for receipts and transactions"
4. "The Feed is for your casual, whenever reads"
5. "Calendaring sucked for years, we fixed it"
6. "And there's so much more"

**Named concepts as brand language:** "The Screener," "The Imbox," "Paper Trail," "The Feed" — proper nouns, not feature descriptions. Product feels like a world with places.

**Founder letter:** Jason Fried's personal letter as a page section. Short paragraphs, reads like an email. "Email gets a bad rap, but it shouldn't. Email's a treasure." — redemption arc narrative.

### Kvalt application
- **Name the concepts.** "The Grill," "Characters," "Token Playground" — proper nouns create a world, not a feature list.
- **One concept per scroll section.** Full-width moment per feature, no cramming into card grids.
- **Founder voice section.** "Why I built this" — personal, direct, builds trust with solo-dev audience.

## Visual Design

- **Typography:** Mix of editorial serif hero + hand-drawn/brush display for section headers. Warmth and personality.
- **Color:** Purple-to-coral gradient hero. Blue accent for links. Warm, not corporate.
- **Spacing:** Generous vertical — each feature gets almost a full viewport.
- **Testimonial cards:** Soft purple/lilac backgrounds with white text.

### Kvalt application
- Gradient hero with warm palette (salmon, lavender) for instant memorability.
- Hand-drawn/editorial typography in section headers aligns with watercolor illustration direction.

## Navigation / IA

- **5 nav items only:** Features, Calendar, For domains, Pricing, FAQs + Sign in/CTA.
- **Footer as philosophy map:** 24 links as flat list. Names are statements, not categories:
  - "It's none of their business" (privacy)
  - "No spy pixel tracking" (anti-practice)
  - "Just let it flow" (workflow philosophy)
  - "Forever yours" (data ownership)
  - "HEY, cool shirt" (merch = brand as lifestyle)

### Kvalt application
- **Footer links as statements.** Instead of "Accessibility" → "Every element works with a keyboard." Instead of "Philosophy" → "Opinions included."
- **Minimal top nav, philosophical depth in footer.**

## Copy / Messaging

**Most opinionated copy of any site analyzed.** Key techniques:

- **Direct attack on incumbents:** "Gmail, Outlook, and Apple got complacent" — names competitors, assigns blame.
- **Informal register:** "Calendaring sucked for years, we fixed it" — "sucked" is deliberate.
- **Invented vocabulary:** "Imbox" — deliberate misspelling becomes memorable brand language.
- **Asterisked emphasis:** "People *really* like HEY" — spoken emphasis in written form.
- **Emotional reframing:** "Email's a treasure" — redemption arc for the category.
- **Short paragraphs:** Founder letter: 1-2 sentences each. Reads like talking, not writing.
- **Warm unexpected sign-off:** "Have a terrific day!" in footer. Not "Copyright 2024."
- **Specific numbers:** "417 times" (not "hundreds of times") feels real.

### Kvalt application
- **Kvalt's voice IS this voice.** Direct, dry, opinionated. Hey proves it works at scale.
- **Invented vocabulary** for Kvalt concepts. Give them proper names.
- **Warm sign-offs** in unexpected places: footer, error states, empty states.
- **Specific numbers** over vague claims: "47 tokens, 24 components, 3 spring personalities."

## Social Proof

- **"People *really* like HEY"** section with 9 named testimonials + photos + links to original tweets.
- **Notable names:** Ryan Hoover (Product Hunt), Ezra Klein (NYT), Adrian Holovaty (Django creator).
- **Bold key phrase in each quote** — skimmers get the gist.
- **Link to original source** — verifiable, not manufactured.
- **Footer:** "Join more than 150,000 people who get our email newsletter" — specific number.
- **Brand provenance:** "HEY is designed, built, and backed by 37signals, the people behind Basecamp."

### Kvalt application
- Bold the key phrase in testimonials. Makes skimming effective.
- Link to original source (tweet, post). Verifiable = trustworthy.
- Brand provenance: "Built by [name], who also built [recognizable thing]."

## Unique Patterns

1. **Rotating social proof ticker above hero** — pre-loads credibility before the pitch.
2. **Named concepts as world-building** — "The Screener," "The Imbox" — product as a place with locations.
3. **Founder letter as page section** — personal, direct, email-style. Not a corporate "About."
4. **Calendar cross-sell banner** — bold coral interruption banner mid-page for secondary product.
5. **Sticky bottom CTA bar** — persistent "Try HEY free" always one click away.
6. **Footer as personality map** — link names are statements and invitations, not categories.
7. **Styled "+" in hero** — "email + calendar" with the + in its own span. Typographic moment.
