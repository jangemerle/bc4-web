# Kvalt Design Voice

What makes a design feel right for Kvalt. Updated from session reactions.

---

## The core personality

**"Unintrusive sparky humour."** — Jan's exact words, 2026-03-31.

Kvalt's humour is:
- **In the concept**, not in the copy. The receipt *is* the joke. The joke isn't written on the receipt.
- **Dry, not loud**. It rewards attention. You smile at it, you don't laugh out loud at it.
- **Functional first**. The design still works perfectly as a design. The humour is a bonus layer.
- **Never precious**. The moment something starts feeling self-congratulatory or ironic-for-irony's-sake, it's gone too far.

## What lands

### The Receipt (V1 — Pricing, 2026-03-31) ✅ LOVE
- Why it works: familiar format (receipt) subverts expectations without announcing it
- Monospace throughout — typeface choice IS the concept
- "CASHIER: THE INTERNET" — humour is embedded in metadata, not headlines
- The concept is immediately readable: you scan it exactly like a receipt
- **Lesson: concepts that use an existing real-world format as the frame work extremely well for Kvalt**

### The Personal Letter (V4 — Pricing, 2026-03-31) ✅ LIKE
- Why it works: intimacy + sincerity land. "Then we deleted all of it." is a great beat.
- Warmth without being saccharine
- **Lesson: genuine directness and warmth work. Not performed warmth.**

### The Terminal (V7/V2 — Pricing & 404, 2026-03-31) ✅ LOVE
- Why it works: the terminal IS the product's native language. Stack traces and CLI output are things the audience lives in.
- "Or just close the tab. That also works." — perfect dry tone
- The interactive layer (hidden Space Invaders on Enter) adds delight without diluting the concept
- **Lesson: formats that match the audience's daily reality resonate hardest. A terminal for a design system audience is home turf.**

## What doesn't land

### The Museum Label (V3 — Pricing, 2026-03-31) ❌ "looks like a parte"
- Why it fails: the self-aware irony becomes the entire point, which tips into pretentious
- "KVALT PERMANENT COLLECTION" feels like a brand that thinks it's more important than it is
- The design is beautiful but the concept is doing too much signalling
- **Lesson: Kvalt shouldn't feel like it's winking at you about how clever it is. The cleverness should be invisible.**

### 404 Round 1 (Receipt, Lost Poster, Parking Ticket, Classified, Departure Board) ❌ "quite poor, super boring"
- Why it fails: repeating the receipt concept doesn't work — it's a one-time frame. Lost poster, parking ticket, classified memo are predictable internet-404 tropes. "First thing that comes to mind" territory.
- The departure board had a nice aesthetic but the concept was surface-level.
- **Lesson: don't repeat a winning format. One receipt is clever, two is a pattern, three is a crutch. Each page needs its OWN conceptual frame.**
- **Lesson: "official document" is not a creative strategy. Ticket, citation, classified — they're all the same joke (authority + mundane). Dig deeper into genuinely unexpected formats: voicemail, grocery shelf, book dedication, CCTV footage.**

### The Newspaper (V5 — Pricing, 2026-03-31) ❌ "too much"
- Why it fails: concept is one layer too deep (you're reading a newspaper to find out the product is free — meta-joke requires too much work)
- Execution gets muddied: columns, classified ads, masthead — quantity of elements dilutes the idea
- **Lesson: complex concepts need simple execution. Simple concepts can handle rich execution. Never both complex concept AND complex execution.**

---

## Principles for Kvalt design

1. **The concept IS the design.** Don't dress up a standard layout with humour. Change the fundamental frame.
2. **Real-world formats work.** Receipt, letter, certificate, ticket, invoice, field notes — these work because they carry inherent meaning.
3. **One good idea, executed cleanly.** Not three good ideas competing.
4. **Dry > Loud.** If you're explaining the joke, it's the wrong joke.
5. **Kvalt green (#7DDB85) as the one accent** — not a palette, one moment of colour.
6. **Negative space is an opinion.** Kvalt has enough confidence to leave things out.
