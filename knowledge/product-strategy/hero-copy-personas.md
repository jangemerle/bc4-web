# Hero Copy — Persona-Based Variations

**Added:** 2026-04-04
**Type:** Marketing copy framework
**Purpose:** Performance-oriented landing page copy, one hero per target persona. Each version leads with the pain that persona feels most.

---

## Copy structure

Each persona gets three pieces:
- **Title** — big, bold, stops the scroll (max 6 words)
- **Subtitle** — one sentence that frames the value proposition for this persona
- **Body** — 2-3 sentences with specifics

---

## Business / Product Manager

> **Title:** Defaults worth keeping.
> **Subtitle:** Decisions included. Meetings not required.
> **Body:** Kvalt ships every design decision pre-made — colors, motion, spacing, typography. Your team stops debating and starts building. One CSS variable to override anything.

**Why this works:** Business people hate process waste. "Meetings not required" is the sharpest promise you can make to someone who lives in calendars.

---

## Developer / Engineer

> **Title:** Defaults worth keeping.
> **Subtitle:** `npm install` and it already looks good.
> **Body:** 25 components, fully animated, accessible out of the box. Every token is named, every opinion is documented. Override anything with one CSS variable — or just ship.

**Why this works:** Devs want it to work immediately. No config, no theming ceremony. The `npm install` framing is their native language.

---

## Designer

> **Title:** Defaults worth keeping.
> **Subtitle:** Every pixel has a reason. Every reason has a page.
> **Body:** Kvalt documents the why behind every design decision — color ratios, motion choreography, spacing rhythm. Figma source of truth included. Disagree with a decision? Override it in one variable and move on.

**Why this works:** Designers care about intentionality. "Every pixel has a reason" signals this isn't another generic kit — it's a system with taste.

---

## CTO / Technical Leader

> **Title:** Defaults worth keeping.
> **Subtitle:** One system. Zero design debt.
> **Body:** Opinionated tokens, animated components, accessibility baked in. Your team ships consistent UI without a dedicated design system team. Every decision is documented — onboarding takes minutes, not sprints.

**Why this works:** CTOs worry about maintenance cost and team velocity. "Zero design debt" + "no DS team needed" hits both.

---

## CEO / Founder

> **Title:** Defaults worth keeping.
> **Subtitle:** Ship the product. The pixels are handled.
> **Body:** Your product looks like a funded company built it — because the design decisions are already made. Kvalt gives small teams the visual quality of a 50-person design org. Focus on the business.

**Why this works:** CEOs don't care about tokens or CSS variables. They care about looking credible and moving fast.

---

## Vibe Coder / Non-Developer

> **Title:** Defaults worth keeping.
> **Subtitle:** You build it. We make it look intentional.
> **Body:** AI tools give you the code. Kvalt gives you the taste. Every component is animated, every color is chosen, every spacing decision is made. Your app stops looking AI-generated and starts looking designed.

**Why this works:** Vibe coders can feel the gap between their output and "real" products. "Look intentional" bridges that gap without patronizing them.

---

## Shared elements

- **Title stays constant** across personas: "Defaults worth keeping." — it's universally resonant
- **Subtitle rotates** based on persona detection or landing page variant
- **Body** can be swapped or A/B tested independently
- **Tone rules:** No exclamation marks. No "simply" or "just" or "easy". Active voice. Important word last.
