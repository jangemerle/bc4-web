## Who is Mo Junayed
Creative strategist and marketing educator specializing in data-driven positioning frameworks. Uses Claude Cowork as the core research and creative automation engine.

---

## Key Takeaways
### 1. Four-Bucket Research Framework
Exhaustive brand analysis across: (1) how the brand positions itself, (2) what actual customers say (Reddit, Amazon, TrustPilot, Facebook groups), (3) competitive landscape, (4) market gaps and opportunities.

### 2. Breakthrough Advertising as Training Ground
Uses Eugene Schwartz's 5 stages of awareness and market sophistication levels as the mental model. Train Cowork on these frameworks — Claude becomes far more nuanced in positioning advice.

### 3. Creative Bible as System of Record
Build an 11-sheet Excel document: customer profiles, awareness stages, 100+ hooks, objection handling, language bank, proof points, angles. This becomes the single source of truth for all messaging.

### 4. Priming Method Over Direct Filling
Step-by-step journeys with sub-steps produce dramatically better results than asking Claude to fill an entire document at once. Build incrementally, validate each layer.

### 5. Self-Check for AI Hallucination
After Claude generates messaging, run a self-check prompt: "Does this sound like AI? Is it shallow? Is it inaccurate?" This catches missed insights and stilted language.

### 6. Reusable Cowork Skills
Entire process converts into a brand-agnostic Cowork Skill that runs autonomously on any new client — 30-min setup unlocks hours of manual research.

## Relevance to Kvalt

- **Documentation as Research Template:** Kvalt's `docs/philosophy.md` and `docs/conventions.md` follow the same "Creative Bible" pattern — a single source of truth for tone, motion principles, and accessibility rules that guides all design decisions.

- **Cowork Skill Pattern Match:** The `.skills/figma-to-kvalt/SKILL.md` skill mirrors Junayed's reusable Creative Bible approach — it trains on Kvalt's token system once, then applies that knowledge to any Figma file without re-specification.

- **Priming in Component Building:** When creating new Kvalt components, use a priming-first approach: clarify intent and constraints (like asking awareness-stage questions), then write specs, then build. This mirrors Junayed's step-by-step journey method.

- **Language Consistency:** Kvalt's design system would benefit from a "voice language bank" (Junayed's term) — a curated set of component naming conventions, prop descriptions, and error messages that all sound cohesive and on-brand.
