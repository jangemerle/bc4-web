## Key Takeaways

The traditional SEO metric of domain rating has become largely irrelevant in the LLM citation era. By reverse-engineering which sources appear in Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and Grok responses, creators can identify high-opportunity search intents and craft content optimized for AI citation rather than ranking. The workflow is straightforward: test your target prompts in each LLM, observe which competitors consistently surface, analyze *why* those sources are cited (content depth, structure, credibility signals), then write substantively better content on the same intent. Domain rating 6–28 sources get cited alongside authority sites — the quality of the answer matters more than the domain's historical SEO weight.

Synonymous search prompts (conceptually similar questions asked different ways) yield the same citations across LLMs. This means the opportunity isn't just "rank for one keyword"—it's to own an *intent*. By identifying all the prompt variations that share the same answer space, creators can target the concept at scale. A master prompt (Claude-generated, iteratively refined) can produce 10x better content than any single cited source, essentially creating the gold standard answer that LLMs will prefer to reference in future responses.

The citation-hunting workflow becomes automatable at scale through autoblog campaigns. Instead of manually testing each intent, creators can orchestrate parallel content sprints across multiple intents and languages, track which sources get cited per intent, and continuously feed that signal back into content generation. The economics work because LLM citations don't require backlinks, ads, or link juice—only that the content is discoverable (indexed, semantically relevant, and substantively better than existing answers in the LLM's training data).
