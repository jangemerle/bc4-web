## What works for dev tool YouTube

### Format performance (ranked)
1. **"I built X with Y" demos** (8-15 min) — Highest engagement. "I built a SaaS in 30 min with Claude Code" format is proven and trending. 83% of dev creators now use AI in workflows.
2. **Comparison/vs videos** (10-15 min) — Naturally high CTR. "shadcn vs Untitled UI" or "Kvalt face vs generic shadcn."
3. **Design decision walkthroughs** (8-12 min) — Underserved niche. Most content is "how to code X" — almost nobody explains "why I designed it this way." This is Kvalt's unique angle.
4. **Before/after transformations** (5-8 min) — Visual, shareable, perfect for face concept demos.
5. **Tutorials** (10-20 min) — Bread and butter but highly competitive. Better as support content than primary draw.

### Optimal specs
- **Length sweet spot:** 7-15 minutes for tutorials, 5-8 for demos. 50%+ retention matters more than length.
- **Posting frequency:** 1-2x/week is sufficient. Consistency beats volume. Kevin Powell posts 2-3x/week, Fireship 1-2x.
- **Best days:** Tuesday + Friday, 10am local time.
- **Title patterns:** Numbers + specific tech + outcome. "5 Design Token Mistakes That Make Your UI Look Generic" > "How to Use Design Tokens."
- **Thumbnail:** High contrast, 3-4 words max, face optional for dev content, before/after split works well.
- **CTR benchmarks:** 4-6% average, 7%+ good, 10%+ exceptional.

### Monetization reality
- **At 10K subs:** Mostly product sales and affiliate. $100-500/month realistic.
- **At 50K subs:** Sponsorships start ($500-1.5K per video). Product sales compound.
- **At 100K subs:** $800-3K per sponsored video. Tech niche pays 5-15x CPM.
- **Primary income for dev tool creators:** Product sales > sponsorships > ads.

### Channels to study
- **Fireship (4.1M)** — Master of short, dense, opinionated content. "100 seconds of X" format.
- **Theo/t3.gg (494K)** — Opinionated takes on dev tools. High engagement through controversy.
- **Kevin Powell (995K)** — CSS specialist. Consistent, educational, search-optimized.
- **UI Collective** — Design systems specifically. Smaller but targeted audience.
- **The Design Project** — Design process content, vibe coding angle.
