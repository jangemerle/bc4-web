# Design Engineer as Primary Persona

**Added:** 2026-04-06
**Type:** Customer persona analysis

---

## Why design engineers are Kvalt's ideal customer

Design engineers ship the full stack from Figma to production. They care about motion, typography, spacing, accessibility, copy — all the things Kvalt has opinions on. They're the person who *would* build Kvalt themselves, but rebuilds it for every project they join.

## The pain

- Join a new team/project every 6-18 months
- Each time rebuild the same foundation — motion tokens, type scale, dark mode, component animation
- Know what good looks like but spend 60% of time on infrastructure instead of interesting design engineering problems
- shadcn gives them components but not the *system* — no choreography, no voice, no personality

## Why Kvalt fits

| Design engineer need | Kvalt answer |
|---|---|
| Motion system day one | Springs, choreography rules, usePress |
| Dark mode that isn't an afterthought | OKLCH-native, token-driven, automatic |
| Convince team on design decisions | Every decision documented with rationale |
| Override things their way | One CSS variable per opinion |
| Ship fast but with taste | Core value proposition |

## Key insight

Design engineers don't want *less* opinion — they want *good* opinions they can modify. shadcn's "no opinion" means they rebuild the opinion layer every time. Kvalt gives a starting opinion that's close enough to tweak, not rebuild.

## Pricing position

Design engineers understand the value — they know what 80 hours of motion system design costs. Would pay more than other personas.

```
Design Engineer tier:  $199-349 one-time
  → everything in Pro
  → character creator (build client themes)
  → Figma source file access
  → spec export (hand off to other devs)
```

## Copy angles for this persona

- "The design system you'd build yourself. Already built."
- "Start with taste. Override with yours."
- "Your first 80 hours of design decisions — decided."

## Product features they'd value most

1. **Character creator** — build a face per client project
2. **Spec export** — hand decisions to junior devs with rationale
3. **Figma source** — bidirectional, not just code
4. **Documented "why"** — ammunition for stakeholder conversations
5. **Motion system** — the hardest part to build from scratch

## Relationship to other personas

- Agency devs and vibe coders buy the **output**
- Design engineers buy the **thinking**
- This may be the primary persona, not secondary — they have highest willingness to pay and strongest word-of-mouth influence

---

## Relevance to Kvalt

Design engineers validate the entire Kvalt thesis: opinionated defaults + easy overrides + documented rationale. They're the customer who understands exactly what they're paying for and would champion it to their teams. Product and copy strategy should treat them as primary, not tertiary.
