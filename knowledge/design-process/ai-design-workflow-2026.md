## The Framework: Where AI Helps, Where It Hurts

**AI is strong at expansion, weak at validation.** Use AI to broaden the problem space, explore variations, and test assumptions. Use humans for the critical thinking that validates whether those explorations solved the real problem.

## Where AI Adds Value

**Market validation research.** AI can analyze competitor positioning, market trends, and customer language at scale. Ask it: "What are competitors doing here? What's the market narrative? Who's winning and why?" This expands your understanding before design starts. Primary research (talking to users) remains non-negotiable, but AI fills the gap for secondary research fast.

**Realistic microcopy.** Figma's AI Replace Content feature can generate context-aware placeholder text. Instead of generic "Click here," it generates realistic error messages, button labels, and empty states that match your domain. This surfaces copy problems during design instead of discovering them during implementation.

**Complex interaction prototyping.** Figma Make lets you design interactions that would take hours to prototype manually. Use it for exploratory interactions — gesture flows, state transitions, system responses. The AI can generate 3–5 variations quickly, which you then evaluate and refine.

## Where AI Hurts (Don't Skip This)

**Primary user research.** Never skip talking to actual users. AI research is secondhand interpretation. If you design based on AI's market analysis without validating with users, you'll miss the real problem. The gap between "what the market says" and "what users actually need" is where good design happens.

**Critical judgment.** AI can generate options; you must choose the right one. This requires taste, domain expertise, and understanding of constraints. Treat AI as a brainstorm partner, not a decision-maker.

## The Startup Workflow

For early-stage products where iteration speed matters, the pattern is tight: **Idea → Prompt → Prototype → Evaluate across 3–5 versions → Iterate.**

Instead of designing one perfect mockup, ask AI to generate 3–5 distinct approaches. Evaluate them against your design principles and user feedback. Iterate on the winning direction. This compresses cycles that would normally take weeks into days.

**Key discipline: Document each iteration.** Write down what changed and why. This prevents iteration from becoming random exploration. The documentation also becomes onboarding for new team members and ammunition for stakeholder conversations.

## Relevance to Kvalt

Kvalt's token system and motion guidelines are exactly the documentation that makes this workflow possible. Instead of each team rediscovering what "good" spacing or motion looks like, Kvalt documents those decisions upfront. This lets teams use AI (Claude Code + Figma) to explore variations within a known-good constraint system — the best of both worlds: AI's speed + design system's taste.
