# Design Has Never Been More in Demand. So Why Can't Juniors Get Hired?

**Source:** State of Play — Tommy Geoco ft. Josh Puckett (YouTube)
**Added:** 2026-03-31
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tNEcRJKgys0

Josh Puckett — ex-Dropbox (under Soleio), built design at Wealthfront, mentor/investor via Upper Study and Combine VC, founder of Interface Craft.

---

## Key Takeaways

### 1. The Junior Paradox
Companies want experienced designers who are great with AI. Junior roles are evaporating — yet design demand has never been higher. The bar is rising, not shrinking.

### 2. Uncommon Effort + Uncommon Taste
Josh's hiring framework: demonstrate **uncommon effort** (volume, prolificness, quantity of output) or **uncommon taste** (aesthetic judgment, creative decisions) — ideally both. The chef analogy: if you want to work at a Michelin-starred restaurant, cook many dishes daily, not one recipe per week.

### 3. Playgrounds Over Portfolios
The classic 3-case-study portfolio gets pattern-matched and skipped in 1 second. What stands out: personal websites with interactive playgrounds that show creativity and earnestness. Named examples: Ria Lu, Chloe Yang, Soren Blank, Lele Zeng.

### 4. "Phantom Competency"
Tommy's concept: if you operate at an AI-assisted ceiling long enough, your real floor rises — you internalize patterns through repetition. Josh agrees LLMs are great tutors but lack specificity at the top end of craft methodology.

### 5. AI Native Is a Moment, Not an Identity
Like "mobile designer" was a differentiator for a few years post-iPhone, "AI native" will become assumed baseline. Focus on universal frameworks, principles, and design practice — not tools.

### 6. Uncommon Care
"What makes a great designer is just giving a shit." Stripe Press did 60-70 iterations on a single book cover concept. Going a little further than default is what separates great work.

### 7. Designer Burnout with AI
Designers are the least happy function with AI because design historically had the least responsibility in the EPD (Engineering-Product-Design) triangle. Building with AI forces confronting the same complexity engineers always dealt with.

### 8. Shot Selection
"It's hard to compete with somebody who's having fun." Follow your excitement, not obligation.

### 9. Design Is Not Art
Product design is always in service of a business problem. Developing empathy for founders and engineers makes you a better teammate.

### 10. Interface Craft
Josh built it as a side project over 4 weeks using Claude ("goblin mode"). Creative onboarding: pick a library card, sign your name, insert it to unlock. Membership community focused on design craft.

## Relevance to Kvalt

- **Playgrounds > portfolios** directly validates the Kvalt styleguide-as-creative-workspace vision. The docs site itself should be an interactive playground that demonstrates taste and craft — not just documents components.
- **Uncommon care** = the 60-70 iteration mindset. This is the ethos behind Kvalt's motion tokens, gamut curves, and per-pixel attention to detail. The DS itself is a demonstration of care.
- **Phantom competency** supports the face system — designers using well-crafted faces repeatedly internalize good patterns (spacing, motion, color relationships), raising their floor over time.
- **AI native is a moment** — don't position Kvalt as "the AI design system." Position it as a great design system that happens to distribute beautifully through AI-native channels (CLI, MCP, faces).
- **Uncommon effort** as hiring signal — Kvalt's volume of components, tokens, and documentation is itself a portfolio piece demonstrating prolific output.
